*Captain's Log, Colony Year 17*

The freighter Persephone drifted in the shadow of an abandoned orbital ring, its hull scarred from a brush with a rogue asteroid field. I had traded the predictable lanes of the Frontier for one last run into the uncharted region beyond the Settled Systems, chasing rumors of lost Helios-class relics and a crew's whispered promise of fortune.

The planet below glowed with cobalt storms, and every sensor ping felt like a heartbeat. We were a ragtag crew of outcasts and prospectors, bound by the same hunger for a new beginning. Mara, our pilot, joked that the only thing more dangerous than the drift was the governor's bounty on unlicensed salvage. I smiled anyway, because out here the only law we truly answered to was the vacuum.

When the Persephone finally touched down on the rusted surface of the derelict station, a stillness fell over the airlock. The station's corridors hummed with forgotten power and the ghosts of a civilization that had once dreamt bigger than the stars. We moved through the silence, our lights cutting through the dark like wayward comets.

At the center chamber, beneath a shattered cryo-lattice, was the artifact everyone had whispered about: a polished sphere the color of deep space. It pulsed faintly, almost alive. In that moment, I understood why so many had risked everything to reach this place. The Persephone's crew had come for salvage and a story, but we left with something far more fragile and dangerous—a spark of hope that the galaxy still held secrets worth fighting for.

*— Captain Rhea Vale, crew of the Persephone*
